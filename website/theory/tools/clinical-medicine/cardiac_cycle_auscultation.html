Heart Sounds

S1
Mitral + Tricuspid closure

Loudest at apex. Corresponds to QRS onset. Marks the beginning of systole.

M1 (mitral) occurs just before T1 (tricuspid) — normally fused into single sound.

Loud S1: Mitral stenosis (valve still wide open at onset), short PR, high cardiac output.

Soft S1: MR (valve already partially closed), long PR, severe MS (calcified immobile valve).

S2
Aortic + Pulmonic closure

Loudest at base (2nd ICS). A2 occurs before P2 (left-sided pressures higher). Marks end of systole.

S3
Early diastolic — rapid ventricular filling

Low-pitched. Best heard at apex with bell, patient in left lateral decubitus.

Normal in: children, young adults, pregnancy (high-flow states).

Pathological >40 yrs: Volume overload — HF (dilated ventricle, high LVEDP), severe MR, high-output states.

"Ken-TUC-ky" — S1 S2 S3
S4
Late diastolic / presystolic — atrial contraction into stiff ventricle

Low-pitched. Best heard at apex with bell. Occurs just before S1.

Always pathological. Stiff, non-compliant ventricle: LVH (from HTN), HCM, aortic stenosis, acute MI.

Absent in atrial fibrillation (no organized atrial contraction = no S4).

"Ten-ne-SSEE" — S4 S1 S2

Aortic Area

Location: 2nd right intercostal space, right sternal border

Best for:

Aortic Stenosis Aortic Regurgitation

Ejection systolic murmur of AS heard loudest here, radiates to carotids. Use diaphragm.

Pulmonic Area

Location: 2nd left intercostal space, left sternal border

Best for:

Pulmonic Stenosis Pulmonic Regurgitation

Pulmonic flow murmurs common in young, high-output states. P2 component of S2 best heard here.

Erb's Point

Location: 3rd left intercostal space, left sternal border

Best for:

AR (radiation) AS (radiation)

Convergence zone for aortic murmurs. Early diastolic murmur of AR often best heard here with patient sitting up, leaning forward, end-expiration.

Tricuspid Area

Location: Lower left sternal border (4th-5th ICS)

Best for:

Tricuspid Regurgitation Tricuspid Stenosis VSD

Right-sided murmurs increase with inspiration (Carvallo sign). TR: pansystolic, increases with inspiration.

Mitral / Apex

Location: 5th left intercostal space, mid-clavicular line

Best for:

Mitral Regurgitation Mitral Stenosis S3 S4 MVP click

MR: pansystolic, radiates to axilla, diaphragm. MS: low-pitched rumble, use bell in left lateral decubitus. S3 & S4 best heard here with bell.
