Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Thyroid Axis

Hypothalamus
↓ TRH
Anterior Pituitary
↓ TSH
Thyroid Gland
↓ T4 (90%) + T3 (10%)
Peripheral Tissues
T4 → T3 (by Deiodinase)

↑ T3/T4 → Negative Feedback → ↓ TRH + TSH
T3 is the active hormone — 3-5x more potent than T4.
T4 serves as a circulating reservoir, converted peripherally to T3 by 5'-deiodinase.
>99% of T3/T4 is protein-bound (TBG); only free fractions are biologically active.

Thyroid

Hyperthyroidism Workup Algorithm

1
Confirm: TSH ↓, T3/T4 ↑
▾
Low TSH with elevated free T4 (and/or free T3) confirms thyrotoxicosis.
T3 thyrotoxicosis: elevated T3 with normal T4 — can be early Graves' or toxic adenoma.
2
Radioactive Iodine Uptake (RAIU)
▾
Diffuse High Uptake
Graves' Disease — TSI/TRAb positive, diffuse goiter, ophthalmopathy, pretibial myxedema
Focal High Uptake
Toxic Adenoma — Single hot nodule, rest of gland suppressed
Patchy Uptake
Toxic MNG — Multiple autonomous nodules
Low / Absent Uptake
Thyroiditis (subacute/de Quervain, postpartum, painless), exogenous thyroid hormone, ectopic thyroid tissue (struma ovarii)
3
Treatment
▾
Graves' Disease
Antithyroid drugs: Carbimazole / Methimazole — block TPO (thyroperoxidase).
PTU: also blocks peripheral T4→T3 conversion. Preferred in 1st trimester and thyroid storm.
Radioactive iodine (RAI) or surgery for definitive treatment.
Thyroid Storm (Emergency)
1. PTU (loading dose) — blocks synthesis + peripheral conversion
2. Beta-blockers (propranolol) — symptomatic control
3. Steroids (hydrocortisone) — block T4→T3, adrenal support
4. Iodine (Lugol's) — AFTER antithyroid drugs (Wolff-Chaikoff effect)
5. Supportive (cooling, fluids, treat precipitant)

Cushing's Syndrome Workup

1
Screening (any 2 of 3 positive)
▾
24-hour urinary free cortisol — elevated (>3x ULN highly specific)
Late-night salivary cortisol — elevated (loss of diurnal nadir)
1 mg overnight dexamethasone suppression test — 8 AM cortisol NOT suppressed (remains >1.8 µg/dL)
2
Determine ACTH Dependence
▾
ACTH Suppressed (<5 pg/mL)
ACTH-Independent → Adrenal source
CT adrenals → adenoma, carcinoma, bilateral hyperplasia
ACTH Elevated / Normal
ACTH-Dependent → Pituitary vs Ectopic → proceed to Step 3
3
Localize ACTH Source
▾
High-dose dexamethasone suppression test (8 mg):
Suppresses (>50% drop)
Cushing Disease (pituitary adenoma) → MRI pituitary
Does NOT suppress
Ectopic ACTH — small cell lung carcinoma, bronchial carcinoid, thymic carcinoid → CT chest/abdomen
IPSS (Inferior Petrosal Sinus Sampling): Gold standard when high-dose dex is equivocal. Central-to-peripheral ACTH ratio >2 (basal) or >3 (after CRH) → pituitary source.
Adrenal

Primary Adrenal Insufficiency (Addison's Disease)

Hormone Pattern
↓ Cortisol, ↓ Aldosterone, ↑ ACTH (loss of negative feedback)
Clinical Features
Hyperpigmentation (↑ACTH → ↑MSH, shared POMC precursor)
Fatigue, weight loss, orthostatic hypotension
Hyperkalemia + hyponatremia (aldosterone loss)
Salt craving, hypoglycemia
Diagnosis
ACTH stimulation test (Synacthen): Cortisol fails to rise >18 µg/dL (or >500 nmol/L) at 30/60 min
Causes
Developed world: Autoimmune adrenalitis (#1) — anti-21-hydroxylase antibodies
Developing world: Tuberculosis (#1)
Acute: Adrenal hemorrhage — Waterhouse-Friderichsen (meningococcal sepsis), anticoagulants

Secondary Adrenal Insufficiency (Pituitary)

Hormone Pattern
↓ Cortisol, ↓ ACTH, Aldosterone PRESERVED (RAAS-regulated, not ACTH-dependent)
Key Differences from Primary
NO hyperpigmentation (ACTH is low)
NO hyperkalemia (aldosterone intact)
Most common cause: Chronic steroid use → sudden withdrawal

Adrenal Crisis (Emergency)

Precipitants
Stress/surgery/illness in undiagnosed Addison's
Sudden steroid withdrawal in chronic steroid user
Bilateral adrenal hemorrhage
Features
Cardiovascular shock, hypoglycemia, hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, fever, altered consciousness
Treatment
1. IV Hydrocortisone 100 mg stat then 50 mg q6-8h
2. IV Normal Saline (aggressive fluid resuscitation)
3. IV Dextrose if hypoglycemic
4. Identify and treat precipitant
No need for separate mineralocorticoid — high-dose hydrocortisone has mineralocorticoid activity

Quick Drill — 10 Scenarios

0/10
scenarios reviewed
1TSH elevated, free T4 low, anti-TPO antibodies positive
Hashimoto's Thyroiditis (Primary Hypothyroidism)
Most common cause of hypothyroidism in iodine-sufficient areas. Anti-TPO and anti-thyroglobulin antibodies. Lymphocytic infiltration of thyroid.
2TSH suppressed, free T4 elevated, diffuse goiter, exophthalmos
Graves' Disease
TSI/TRAb stimulates TSH receptor. Classic triad: hyperthyroidism + ophthalmopathy + pretibial myxedema. Diffuse high RAIU.
3TSH suppressed, free T4 elevated, low RAIU, tender thyroid
Subacute Thyroiditis (de Quervain's)
Viral post-URI, painful tender thyroid, elevated ESR. Self-limiting: thyrotoxic phase → hypothyroid phase → recovery. Low RAIU because gland is inflamed, not overproducing.
4Cortisol not suppressed by 1 mg dex, ACTH elevated, suppresses with high-dose dex
Cushing Disease (Pituitary Adenoma)
ACTH-dependent Cushing's where high-dose dex suppresses the pituitary adenoma. MRI pituitary for microadenoma. IPSS if imaging equivocal.
5Cortisol elevated, ACTH suppressed, adrenal mass on imaging
Adrenal Cushing's (Adenoma/Carcinoma)
ACTH-independent: autonomous cortisol production by adrenal tumor suppresses pituitary ACTH. CT adrenals. Carcinoma if large (>4 cm), irregular, or mixed hormone secretion.
6Hyperpigmentation, hyperkalemia, low cortisol, high ACTH
Addison's Disease (Primary Adrenal Insufficiency)
High ACTH (loss of feedback) → MSH co-secretion → hyperpigmentation. Aldosterone loss → hyperkalemia + hyponatremia. Confirm with ACTH stimulation test.
7Patient on long-term prednisolone, abrupt discontinuation, now hypotensive and confused
Adrenal Crisis (Secondary Insufficiency)
Chronic exogenous steroids suppress HPA axis. Sudden withdrawal → adrenal crisis. Treatment: IV hydrocortisone 100 mg stat + fluids. Taper steroids gradually.
8TSH low, free T4 low, panhypopituitarism
Central/Secondary Hypothyroidism
TSH is inappropriately low/normal for a low T4 — pituitary failure. Causes: pituitary adenoma, Sheehan's, surgery, radiation. TSH is unreliable for monitoring; follow free T4.
9Cortisol high, ACTH high, no suppression with high-dose dex, small cell lung cancer
Ectopic ACTH Syndrome
SCLC produces ACTH autonomously — not suppressible by high-dose dex. Also: bronchial carcinoid, thymic carcinoid, pheochromocytoma. Often presents with hypokalemic alkalosis.
10TSH normal but high free T4 and T3, no symptoms of hyperthyroidism
Thyroid Hormone Resistance (RTH)
Mutation in thyroid hormone receptor beta (TR-beta). Tissues resistant to T3/T4 → compensatory ↑TSH → ↑T3/T4. Usually asymptomatic. Differentiate from TSH-oma by family history and alpha-subunit levels.
