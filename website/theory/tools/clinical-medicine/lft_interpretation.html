Quick Categories

Hepatocyte injury markers: ALT, AST
Cholestasis markers: ALP, GGT, Bilirubin
Synthetic function: Albumin, PT/INR

Key principle: Compare the degree of ALT/AST elevation vs ALP elevation to determine the pattern. Tap each step below.

1
ALT/AST >> ALP → Hepatocellular Hepatocyte damage predominates
▼
AST/ALT >1000: Acute viral hepatitis, drug-induced (paracetamol), ischemic hepatitis ("shock liver"), autoimmune hepatitis
AST/ALT 300–1000: Acute viral hepatitis, autoimmune hepatitis
AST:ALT >2:1 → Alcoholic liver disease
Classic "2:1 ratio" — AST rarely exceeds 300 in alcoholic hepatitis.
Mechanism: Alcohol depletes hepatic pyridoxal-5-phosphate (B6 cofactor for ALT)
AST:ALT ≈ 1:1 → Most other causes of hepatitis
2
ALP >> ALT/AST → Cholestatic Bile flow obstruction
▼
Intrahepatic: PBC (AMA positive), drug-induced cholestasis, infiltrative disease (sarcoid, lymphoma, amyloid), intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy
Extrahepatic: CBD stone, pancreatic head cancer, cholangiocarcinoma, PSC (p-ANCA positive)
Confirm hepatic origin of ↑ALP: Check GGT
GGT ↑ = Hepatic origin  |  GGT normal = Bone origin (e.g., Paget's, osteomalacia)
3
Both elevated → Mixed Pattern Proportional elevation of both
▼
Consider: Drug-induced liver injury, granulomatous hepatitis, hepatic infiltration
Many drugs can cause mixed patterns — always take a thorough drug history

Bilirubin Metabolism Pathway

1
Heme breakdown (spleen) → Unconjugated bilirubin (indirect, bound to albumin, lipid-soluble)
↓
2
Liver uptake + Conjugation — UDP-glucuronyl transferase → Conjugated bilirubin (direct, water-soluble)
↓
3
Excretion into bile → Gut bacteria convert to urobilinogen
↓
4
Urobilinogen: Reabsorbed (enterohepatic circulation) OR excreted in stool (stercobilin — brown color) OR urine (urobilinogen)

High-Yield Distinction

Conjugated (direct) bilirubin is water-soluble → appears in urine ("dark urine / cola-colored").
Unconjugated (indirect) bilirubin is albumin-bound → does NOT appear in urine.

If urine bilirubin is positive → the problem is at or beyond the liver (hepatocellular or obstructive).

Acute Viral Hepatitis
Hepatocellular — ALT/AST >1000
ALT/AST: ↑↑↑ (>1000)   ALP/GGT: Mild ↑
Bilirubin: ↑   Albumin: N (early)   PT: N (early)
Key clue: Anti-HAV IgM, HBsAg, anti-HCV antibody
Alcoholic Hepatitis
AST:ALT >2:1 — AST rarely >300
ALT/AST: ↑ (AST:ALT >2)   ALP/GGT: ↑
Bilirubin: ↑   Albumin: ↓   PT: ↑
Key clue: AST rarely >300, calculate Maddrey Discriminant Function
Paracetamol Toxicity
Hepatocellular — ALT/AST >3000
ALT/AST: ↑↑↑ (>3000)   ALP/GGT: N/mild ↑
Bilirubin: ↑ (late)   Albumin: ↓ (late)   PT: ↑↑
Key clue: N-acetylcysteine (NAC), check paracetamol levels, Rumack-Matthew nomogram
NAFLD / NASH
Mild elevation — ALT > AST
ALT/AST: Mild ↑ (ALT > AST)   ALP/GGT: N/mild ↑
Bilirubin: N   Albumin: N   PT: N
Key clue: Metabolic syndrome, fatty liver on ultrasound
Primary Biliary Cholangitis (PBC)
Cholestatic — AMA positive
ALT/AST: N/mild ↑   ALP/GGT: ↑↑
Bilirubin: ↑ (late)   Albumin: ↓ (late)   PT: N
Key clue: AMA positive, middle-aged female, pruritus
Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC)
Cholestatic — beading on MRCP
ALT/AST: N/mild ↑   ALP/GGT: ↑↑
Bilirubin: ↑ (late)   Albumin: ↓ (late)   PT: N
Key clue: p-ANCA positive, beading on MRCP, association with IBD (UC)
Autoimmune Hepatitis
Hepatocellular — ANA, anti-SMA
ALT/AST: ↑↑   ALP/GGT: N/mild ↑
Bilirubin: ↑   Albumin: ↓   PT: ↑
Key clue: ANA, anti-smooth muscle antibody (SMA), elevated IgG, young female
Wilson's Disease
Low ceruloplasmin, KF rings
ALT/AST: ↑↑   ALP/GGT: N
Bilirubin: ↑   Albumin: ↓   PT: ↑↑
Key clue: Low ceruloplasmin, Kayser-Fleischer rings, young patient, ALP:Bilirubin ratio <4
Hemochromatosis
High ferritin, high transferrin saturation
ALT/AST: Mild ↑   ALP/GGT: N
Bilirubin: N   Albumin: N (early)   PT: N
Key clue: High ferritin, transferrin saturation >45%, HFE gene mutation, bronze diabetes
