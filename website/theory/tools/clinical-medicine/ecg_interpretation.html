ECG Interpretation Algorithm

Systematic approach to 12-lead ECG reading

Harrison's Ch. 268
0 / 7 steps
1
Rate
▾
Method: 300 ÷ RR interval (number of large boxes between R waves)

60–100 bpm → Normal
<60 bpm → Bradycardia
>100 bpm → Tachycardia

Irregular rhythm? Count QRS complexes in 6 seconds (30 large boxes) × 10.
2
Rhythm
▾
Ask: Regular or irregular? P waves before every QRS? P:QRS ratio 1:1?

Regular + P before QRS → Sinus rhythm
Irregularly irregular → Atrial Fibrillation
Regular, no P waves → Junctional or VT
3
Axis
▾
Quadrant method: Check net QRS deflection in Lead I and aVF

Both positive → Normal axis (−30° to +90°)
I positive, aVF negative → Left axis deviation
I negative, aVF positive → Right axis deviation
Both negative → Extreme axis deviation
4
Intervals
▾

PR Interval

0.12–0.20s → Normal
<0.12s → WPW / preexcitation
>0.20s → 1st degree heart block

QRS Duration

<0.12s → Normal
≥0.12s → BBB, ventricular origin, or preexcitation

QT / QTc

QTc <440ms (men), <460ms (women) → Normal
Prolonged QTc → Risk of Torsades de Pointes
5
P-Wave Morphology
▾

Right Atrial Enlargement (P pulmonale)

Tall peaked P wave >2.5mm in lead II

Left Atrial Enlargement (P mitrale)

Wide notched P wave >0.12s in lead II
Biphasic P wave in V1 (deep terminal negative component)
6
QRS Morphology
▾

Bundle Branch Blocks

RBBB: rsR′ in V1 (“M-shaped”), wide S in V6
LBBB: Absent R in V1, broad notched R in V5–V6

Ventricular Hypertrophy

LVH (Sokolow-Lyon): S in V1 + R in V5/V6 ≥35mm
RVH: R > S in V1, right axis deviation

Reciprocal Changes

ST depression in leads opposite to the infarct territory supports the diagnosis of STEMI over pericarditis.

Inferior STEMI: Reciprocal depression in I, aVL
Anterior STEMI: Reciprocal depression in II, III, aVF
Lateral STEMI: Reciprocal depression in III, aVF
