Key Differentiators

IDA vs ACD: TIBC is high in IDA (body craving iron) but low in ACD (iron trapped in stores). Ferritin <30 is diagnostic for IDA; >100 favors ACD.
Thalassemia: Iron studies are normal/high. Look for target cells, elevated HbA2 (beta-thal), and family history.
Sideroblastic: Ring sideroblasts on Prussian blue stain are pathognomonic.

Low Reticulocyte Count

<2% — Inadequate marrow response

Points to a production problem: nutritional deficiency (iron, B12, folate), bone marrow failure, anemia of chronic disease, renal failure.
